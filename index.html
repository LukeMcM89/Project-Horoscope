A Brief History of Horoscopes

A horoscope is an astrological chart, also commonly referred to as a natal chart or cosmogram, that represents the position of the Sun, Moon, planets, astrological aspects and sensitive angels at the time of an event. An important moment for many being the moment of a persons birth.


The word horoscope is derived from the Greek words ora and scopos meaning "time" and "observer of the hour". Essentially, the horoscope serves as a stylized map of the heavens over a specific location at a particular moment in time. The positions of the planets, including the Sun and Moon are placed in the chart, along with other calculated factor such as the lunar nodes, the house cusps, the zodiac signs and fixed stars. The angular relationships between the planets themselves and other points, called aspects are then determined. It is important to note that the emphasis and interpretation of these factors vary with tradition. So in essence, the stars were placed at the time of birth for a person shows their characteristics and personality, including weakness.
